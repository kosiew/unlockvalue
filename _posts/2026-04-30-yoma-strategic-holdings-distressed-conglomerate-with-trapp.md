**Company:** Yoma Strategic Holdings (Myanmar / Singapore-listed)
**Theme:** Distressed conglomerate with trapped asset value

---

## 1) Situation Overview

Yoma Strategic is a classic **distressed Southeast Asian conglomerate**:

* Exposure to **real estate, infrastructure, and services in Myanmar**
* Once positioned as a frontier growth story
* Now heavily impaired due to:

  * Political instability after the 2021 coup
  * Currency collapse
  * Foreign investor exit

👉 **Result:**

**Market cap has collapsed**, but underlying assets still exist, creating a **“trapped value vs. uninvestable market”** problem.

---

## 2) Business Segments

1. **Real Estate (Land & Developments)**

   * Landmark projects in Yangon
   * Long-dated, capital-intensive, now illiquid

2. **Yoma F&B (KFC, Pizza Hut franchises)**

   * Consumer-facing, more resilient
   * Generates cash but constrained by economy

3. **Wave Money (Mobile Wallet JV)**

   * One of Myanmar’s leading digital payment platforms
   * High strategic value, but regulatory/political risk

4. **Automotive & Others**

   * Smaller, cyclical businesses

---

## 3) Core Problem

This is not just a conglomerate discount.

It’s a **“jurisdiction discount + liquidity trap”**:

* Foreign investors avoid Myanmar exposure entirely
* Assets cannot be easily monetized
* Capital cannot be efficiently deployed or repatriated

👉 Even good businesses, such as Wave Money, are **undervalued by association**.

---

## 4) Value Dislocation: Sum-of-the-Parts Thinking

If assets were hypothetically separable:

| Segment     | Intrinsic Quality         | Realizable Value Today |
| ----------- | ------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Wave Money  | High                      | Severely discounted    |
| F&B         | Moderate, cash-generating | Partially realizable   |
| Real Estate | High long-term            | Near-zero liquidity    |
| Others      | Low                       | Minimal                |

**Key insight:**

The issue is not asset quality alone — it’s **convertibility into shareholder value**.

---

## 5) Restructuring Options

### 🟢 Conservative Option: Survival & Cash Preservation

**What it looks like:**

* Freeze major real estate capex
* Focus on F&B cash generation
* Maintain Wave Money stake without aggressive expansion

**Why this works:**

* Preserves liquidity in an unstable macro environment
* Avoids forced asset sales at distressed prices

**Trade-offs:**

* No near-term value unlock
* Equity story remains “dead money”

---

### 🟡 Moderate Option: Strategic Stake Sales — Externalization of Value

**What it looks like:**

* Sell partial stake in **Wave Money** to regional fintech players or NGOs/DFIs
* Bring in strategic partners for F&B

**Why this works:**

* Extracts **hard currency value** from most attractive asset
* Creates external price discovery

**Challenges:**

* Buyer universe is limited due to sanctions/political risk
* Transactions may require complex structuring, such as offshore vehicles

**Key insight:**

This is the **most realistic path to unlock value** in distressed frontier markets.

---

### 🔴 Aggressive Option: Structural Separation / Asset Migration

**What it looks like:**

* Move valuable assets, such as the Wave Money stake, into **offshore holding structures**
* Gradually reduce Myanmar exposure at the listed entity level
* Potentially transform Yoma into a regional investment vehicle

**Why this works:**

* Separates “good assets” from “bad jurisdiction”
* Makes parts of the business investable again

**Trade-offs:**

* Legally and politically complex
* Risk of regulatory pushback from Myanmar authorities
* Execution risk extremely high

---

## 6) Distressed-Specific Dynamics

This case highlights a key restructuring principle:

> In distressed environments, **liquidity matters more than valuation**.

* A “high-value” asset that cannot be sold has low effective value
* Access to foreign capital becomes the primary constraint

---

## 7) Second-Order Effects

* **Stakeholder Misalignment:**

  * Minority shareholders want exits
  * Local stakeholders prioritize survival

* **Time Arbitrage:**

  * Long-term investors may see deep value
  * But timeline is highly uncertain

* **Optionality vs. Burn Rate:**

  * Holding assets preserves upside
  * But ongoing costs erode equity value

---

## 8) Key Constraints

* Sanctions / compliance risks
* Currency convertibility and kyat volatility
* Limited buyer universe
* Political unpredictability

---

## 9) Strategic Insight

Yoma is not just restructuring a company — it’s restructuring around a **country risk problem**.

> The real question is not “how to improve the business,”
> but **“how to extract value from an uninvestable environment.”**

---

## 10) Likely Path: Realistic View

1. Maintain core operations: F&B and Wave Money
2. Opportunistically sell minority stakes when buyers emerge
3. De-emphasize large-scale real estate development
4. Slowly reposition capital outside Myanmar if possible

---

## 11) Key Question for Management

> Do you optimize for **survival in Myanmar**, or **value extraction for shareholders**?

In distressed frontier markets, those two goals often conflict.
